The Science

Understanding the
Cellular Terrain

NAD+ is the primary electron carrier in cellular energy production — involved in 500+ enzymatic reactions.
It activates sirtuins (longevity proteins) and fuels PARP-mediated DNA repair.
Tissue levels fall approximately 50% between early adulthood and age 60 (Verdin, Science 2015).

Measurably slower tissue repair, decreased mitochondrial output, accelerated biological aging markers.
These are the documented correlates of NAD+ depletion (Yoshino et al., Cell Metabolism 2021).
People experience this as "getting older."

Oral NMN bioavailability: estimated 5–25% depending on formulation and hepatic conversion efficiency.
IV NAD+: >95% bioavailability — bypasses intestinal absorption and first-pass liver metabolism entirely.
Compound reaches target tissues directly, within minutes of infusion.
